My Dearest Pondaaati Kuttiyee,

I want to write you something perfect. Something that, when you read it, makes you feel every single thing I feel when I look at you. But the truth is — I cannot. Not because the words do not exist, but because what I feel for you is not a feeling that fits inside a sentence. It is a fact. Permanent. Bone-deep. The kind of thing that does not need to be said because it simply is.

You changed me. Not in the way people say it casually — you actually, genuinely changed the way I move through this world. You made me softer in the places I had hardened. You made me braver in the places I had given up. Without trying, without even knowing it, you made me into someone I am proud to be — because I wanted to be worthy of you.

I think about the version of my life that never found you, and I cannot breathe. Not because it would have been empty — but because I know now what I would have been missing, and the thought of that kind of blindness is unbearable.

So on your birthday, this is what I want you to know: you are the reason everything feels different now. The reason ordinary moments feel like they matter. The reason I wake up and actually want to be here. You did that. Just by being you, you did that.

Happy Birthday, my love. Every single year with you is something I will never stop being staggered by.

Yours, always & entirely
